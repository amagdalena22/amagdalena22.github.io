Storyline Costumes

Coppelia is a comic ballet choreographed by Marius Petipa or Arthur Saint-Leon with music by Leo Delibes. In the story, Dr. Coppelius makes a dancing and life-sized doll named Coppelia. Coppelia causes a fight between Swanhilda and Franz, who are engaged, when Franz falls in love with Coppelia. When Swanhilda learns Coppelia is a doll, she attempts to imitate Coppelia to win Franz's love back. Dr. Coppelius almost kills Franz, but Swanhilda saves him. In the end, Franz and Swanhilda are reuinted and get married.
This variation occurs at the beginning of the ballet in the village.

  • This costume is often a white dress or tutu with a pastel-colored or green bodice.
  • The hairpiece that Coppelia wears can be ribbons or flowers.
  • The ballet takes place in a small village, so the costume reflects this instead of presenting a royal tutu.
